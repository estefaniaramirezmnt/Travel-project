Why Cáceres?

Cáceres, the city of the three cultures

The history of Cáceres goes way back in time. It is a city that has been nourished by all the cultures that have passed through it. You can find prehistoric sites such as "Las Cuevas de Maltravieso", Roman roads, Arab and medieval buildings. That is why it is called the city of the three cultures, because walking through its old town you can see the vestiges of Medieval, Arab and Roman cultures. But that's not all, Cáceres is also a modern city, with a great cultural offer and a great gastronomic variety.

A medieval market is held every November, and all the city is filled with historic ambience. In May, you can enjoy the WOMAD festival, a multicultural festival that brings together artists from all over the world. And 23rd April San Jorge is celebrated, with a great medieval atmosphere, a theatre show in the Plaza Mayor. At the end of the night, there is a big fight between the Moors and the Christians, and finally San Jorge appears on his white horse, killing a gigantic dragon by setting it on fire. It is a city that has been declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO and also the Spanish Gastronomy Capital.

Places

My favorite places in Cáceres

Arco de la Estrella

EL Arco de la Estrella is the main entrance to the Monumental City. It joins the Plaza Mayor with the Plaza de Santa María, both of which have been the nerve centers of the old and new city for centuries.

Address

Plaza Mayor

What I like about it

It's were the modern city meets the old city, the entrance to another world. You will want a photo here!

How to go

Parque del Príncipe

A lovely space in which we can find a large central pond and a Museum of Outdoor Sculptures. At the main entrance there is a botanical garden and in the final section, a huge and beautiful fountain.

Address

C/ José Luis Cotallo

What I like about it

You can breathe fresh air and enjoy the nature in the heart of the city. It's a perfect place to go for a walk or to do some sport.

How to go

Plaza de San Jorge

Dedicated to the patron saint of Cáceres, San Jorge, it is located in the heart of the Monumental City. It highlights the beautiful staircase that leads to the Church of San Francisco Javier and the school of the Society of Jesus.

Address

Plaza San Jorge

What I like about it

It's a very quiet place, where you can sit and enjoy the views of the ancient city.
